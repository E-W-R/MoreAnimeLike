About MoreAnimeLike.com

Imagine this.

You just finished the best anime of your life and want to keep experiencing the exact same thing but slightly different as many times as you can.

So you search “what are some more anime like Attack on Titan” or “anime to watch if you liked Your Name”.

But all you get are those lame articles with dozens of ads written by people who barely watch anime citing the same generic recommendations over and over with no objective reasoning.

That used to be me, so I fixed the problem.

One website, 2048 anime, 40960 recommendations, no ads, no meandering introductions, and one really good algorithm.


Here’s how it works.

Anime nerds like to keep track of everything they’ve watched. So they turn to sites like MyAnimeList and AniList for logging and rating. This gives us a lot of data to work with.

Too much data, really. So I only looked at 102615 different people’s opinions. Which took a lot of space on my computer.

But yeah. Basically we say that two anime are similar if the people we have data on tended to give them similar ratings.

Nothing complicated, really, but it’s pretty powerful. The effect is that if you liked an anime, you’re very likely to like a corresponding recommendation.

Because the recommendations are based on data and math, not vibes.

And if you’re curious about the math, it’s just the mean absolute deviation of two very large vectors. One vector per anime and one dimension for each person who has seen them both.
